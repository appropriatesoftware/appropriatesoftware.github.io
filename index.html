Appropriate Software Foundation

The Appropriate Software Foundation is an established micro-consultancy for software architecture and development. It is a company registered in England and Wales (#04977110) and limited by guarantee. Our registered address is 34 Market Street, Hyde, Cheshire, SK14 1AH.

Operating since 1999, we support human process with working software. We work internationally, with public and private sector organisations, governmental and non-governmental organisations, international agencies and community based organisations. The Appropriate Software Foundation is incorporated as a non-profit organisation. We do not receive grants. Our staff costs are covered by clients engaging with our consulting services.

In the background, we have been working towards grounding our project work in a universal approach to supporting human process with working software, something everybody can use to approach any shared situation. We want to create a world in which everybody can recognise each other as human developers. Through our experience working across a broad range of domains, we have come to feel the modern and effective approach to developing software and human process is to model and propose "what happens" as "occasions of experience". As Alfred North Whitehead wrote in Process and Reality, "the actual world is built up of actual occasions". It seems to us the difficulty with the classical analysis of object orientation, of the agile manifesto, of the patterns of enterprise application architecture, and even the original version of domain driven design is, on one hand, their involvement with the substance-quality categories, and on the other hand, the failure to understand what is an event. With that recognition, we want to move away classical analysis and using CRUD by default, and also away from the agile manifesto, because even its name invokes the substance-quality categories. There aren't any agile teams or organisations or people or coaches because there is no such thing as an agile event: events merely become and then perish and never really are. In that sense, the Appropriate Software Foundation is currently becoming increasingly event-oriented.

Operating since 1999, we help support human process with working software. We work internationally, with public and private sector organisations, governmental and non-governmental organisations, international agencies and community based organisations. The Appropriate Software Foundation is incorporated as a non-profit organisation. Our staff costs are covered entirely by clients engaging with our consulting services.

We have also been working towards grounding our client work in a universal approach to supporting human process with working software, something everyone can use to improve any shared situation. We want to live in a world where we can relate to each other as world developers. Through our experience working across a broad range of domains, we have come to feel the best approach to the development of software systems and of human process is to model and propose "what happens". As Alfred North Whitehead wrote in Process and Reality, "the actual world is built up of actual occasions". It seems to us the difficulty with the classical analysis of object orientated programming, for example Martin Fowler's Patterns of Enterprise Application Architecture, is, on one hand, the involvement of the substance-quality categories, and on the other hand, the failure to understand the modern process philosophy of events. With that recognition, we want to move away from the classical analysis and using CRUD by default. We also want to move away from the Agile Manifesto, because process is not less valuable than people but is rather the attainment of a final end that makes a person and a software system whatever it happens to be, and because it fails to contribute sufficient determination to events so that even after twenty years we can't be sure quite what will be happening in any given "agile" project. For these reasons, the Appropriate Software Foundation is becoming increasingly event-oriented.
